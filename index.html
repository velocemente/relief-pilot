Importing an OFP

Tap ＋ on the Flights screen to open the import screen. Three methods are supported:

① PDF File: Tap the drop zone or drag an OFP PDF onto it. The app extracts and parses text using PDF.js.

② iOS Share: In Files or Mail, open the OFP PDF → tap Share ↑ → select Relief Pilot. Add Relief Pilot to your Home Screen first to enable this method.

③ Paste Text: Copy OFP text from any source and paste it into the text area at the bottom of the import screen, then tap Import Text.

Flight Screen — Tabs

After a flight is loaded, four tabs appear at the bottom of the screen. The FCIR/ASAP toggle is always visible in the top navigation bar.

Briefing

Three-phase briefing note — Preflight, Enroute, and Descent — selectable with tab pills at the top. Each phase shows the fields relevant to that stage of flight. Editable fields are shown in tint colour with a dotted underline.

Airports

Full airport cards for every airport on the release — origin, destination, all alternates, and CFS alternates. Weather and NOTAMs are auto-populated from the OFP. NOTAM items are organised by runway in numerical order, with a hierarchical tree of closure, approach/navaid, lighting, and procedure items.

OFP

The original OFP rendered page-by-page from the imported PDF. Falls back to raw text if no PDF was imported.

Alternates

Alternate routes parsed from the OFP. Routes are editable and can each be copied to clipboard. ETP coordinates appear at the bottom for ETOPS flights.

Briefing — Preflight Phase

Flight Header

Flight ID: Carrier + flight number + "/" + release date day. Turns yellow when FCIR/ASAP is active.

OUT / IN: OUT is the OFP scheduled off-block time. Editing OUT recalculates all downstream ETAs. IN recalculates when the actual OFF time is entered on the Enroute phase.

ETOPS block (ETOPS flights only): Shows ETOPS authorisation and elapsed time to ETOPS airspace entry, both parsed from the OFP.

Departure

ATIS / PARK: Departure ATIS letter (tap to enter) and parking/gate notepad.

OEI: One Engine Inoperative takeoff procedure. When a TLR is parsed, tap to select the departure runway from the picker. SPECIAL runways show the full EFP text; SEE CO CHART runways open a notepad for crew entry.

STAB: Stabilizer trim setting. Always crew-entered.

Slot Time

When the OFP contains a wheels-up slot constraint, a Slot Time subcard appears showing the window (e.g. 0737Z – 0807Z, NOT BEFORE 0737Z, or BEFORE 0807Z) parsed directly from the OFP remarks.

OFP Fuel

Block: Total fuel at departure. Colour-coded by TOGW margin: green >1,000 · yellow 500–1,000 · red <500 from structural limit.

MIN / REMF: Minimum dispatch fuel and planned remaining fuel at destination.

FF ADJ: Appears when a CDL or MEL item carries an additional fuel flow adjustment (e.g. +2.22%). Shown in yellow for a penalty, green for a benefit.

⚠ MTOW LTD BY VZFW/CK: Appears in red when the OFP indicates takeoff weight is limited by Zero Fuel Weight or Centre of Gravity constraints. Confirm with dispatch before departure.

NOTES Section

Airport status: One compact row per airport (origin, destination, alternates, CFS alternates). Each row shows a colour dot and badge category labels evaluated within the departure/arrival window: NOTAM, WX, CURFEW.

● Green — no issues · ● Yellow — advisory · ● Red — possible conflict

DDG / CDL: MEL and CDL items parsed from the OFP. 25-series (NEF/cabin) items are filtered out. CDL items (C-prefix ATA codes) are marked with a CDL badge. Items with a TLR Takeoff or Both cross-reference carry a coloured TLR badge.

Overflights: Permit numbers parsed from the release. Only shown when present.

Curfews: Airports within 90 minutes of a curfew window listed in yellow or red. Parsed from Company NOTAMs.

Notes: Free-text notepad. Accepts keyboard and Apple Pencil Scribble.

Briefing — Enroute Phase

Enter the actual OFF time (wheels-up) to activate the enroute timeline. All computed times update from that moment.

E.ENT (ETOPS flights): Computed ETOPS airspace entry time based on OFF + OFP elapsed time.

RDA / POR (redispatch flights): Redispatch Action time (2 hrs before POR) and Point of Redispatch with its MINF fuel requirement, both computed from OFF time.

CO RPT: Company position report schedule computed at 4-hour intervals from OFF. Reports that fall within the POR action window are struck through and annotated.

A free-text Scratchpad occupies the right column for calculations, clearances, or ATC instructions.

Briefing — Descent Phase

Airport

Displayed at the top of the descent card. Shows the selected airport's colour-coded status (dot + NOTAM/WX badge summary). When the release includes alternates, a chevron ▾ opens a picker to switch between destination and any alternate.

CAT II/III: If the OFP authorises CAT II or III approaches for the selected airport, the applicable runway list is shown below the status row (e.g. CAT III - RWY 09C/27C).

Arrival

Arrival ATIS letter (tap to enter) and parking/gate notepad, independent of the Preflight departure ATIS.

Arrival Fuel

REMF (planned remaining fuel at destination), ALT 1 fuel, FRES (final reserve), and GA fuel. All parsed from the OFP. GA is crew-entered.

DDG — Landing

DDG/MEL items that carry a TLR Landing or Takeoff & Landing cross-reference are pulled out of the full DDG list and displayed here as an arrival-specific reminder. CDL items are marked with a CDL badge.

A free-text Scratchpad is available for arrival calculations, clearance, or gate notes.

Airports Tab — Detail

Each airport card shows the role (ORIG, DEST, DEST ALT, EN ROUTE ALT, TKOF ALT, CFS ALT), ETA, and a colour-coded status badge from combined weather and NOTAM evaluation.

WX Window: ±1 hour of ETA for all airports. CFS alternates use the OFP-published WX WINDOW. Origin uses OUT time.

Weather: Prevailing ceiling/visibility evaluated against Open / Operational / Red thresholds. TEMPO, PROB40, RVR, windshear, and freezing precipitation are evaluated separately.

NOTAMs — Runway tree: All runways appear in numerical order (04, 09, 10, 22, 27…). Each runway is a collapsible top-level entry. Within a runway, NOTAMs are organised into four priority tiers:

● Closed — runway or airport closure

● Approach / Navaid — ILS, GS, LOC, RNAV, GPS/GNSS, DME, marker beacons

● Runway Lighting — PAPI, VASI, ALS, REIL, RCLL, TDZ, HIRL, MIRL, lead-off lights

● Procedure Changes — IAP amendments, minimums raised, missed approach, STAR/SID changes

When two or more NOTAMs fall in the same tier, they collapse under a count label (e.g. Approach / Navaid (3)). Tap to expand and reveal individual items. Tap any item to show the raw NOTAM text.

Editing: Tap the card header to expand the NOTAM and WX text areas. Paste or edit data here to supplement OFP-extracted content.

Curfew row: Appears when Company NOTAMs contain a curfew window. Shows window times and proximity to ETA, colour-coded.

ETOPS & Critical Fuel Scenario

On ETOPS flights the OFP contains a Critical Fuel Scenario (CFS) section listing ETOPS alternates with ETP pairs and WX windows. These airports are automatically added and receive full airport cards.

Each CFS airport uses the OFP-published WX WINDOW. The Enroute phase shows E.ENT (ETOPS entry time) computed from the actual OFF time.

ETP coordinates (e.g. ETP1 N59 42.2 W036 03.6) are listed on the Alternates tab with a copy-to-clipboard button for each point.

Editing Values

Any value shown in tinted dotted-underline text is editable. Tap to open an edit popover. Editing OUT recalculates all downstream ETAs. Editing the actual OFF time on the Enroute phase recalculates the IN time using OFP trip time.

Airport weather and NOTAM fields on each airport card are also editable — tap the card header to expand the input areas.

FCIR / ASAP Toggle

The FCIR/ASAP toggle sits in the navigation bar at the top of the flight screen. When active, the flight ID turns yellow and the flight row in the Saved Flights list stays highlighted until the toggle is turned off.

Activating the toggle reveals a FCIR/ASAP Narrative notepad on every phase. Record event details there and tap the copy icon to copy to clipboard for your reporting system.

⚠ Do not delete this flight entry until the report has been submitted. The flight cannot be deleted while the toggle is ON.

To allow deletion: open the flight, turn off the toggle, then return to Saved Flights.
